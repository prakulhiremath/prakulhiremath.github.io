Industrial Carbon Utilization

Convert CO₂ Into Revenue

Industrial-scale microalgal biorefinery. Transform mandatory carbon compliance into a profitable operation.

The Challenge

India's Carbon Compliance Crisis

Industrial carbon intensity targets are mandatory. But no system makes compliance profitable.

1
Compliance Pressure
CCTS enforcement (FY2026+) forces 700+ industrial facilities to reduce CO₂ intensity or buy credits at ₹200–1,500/tonne.
2
No Profitable Path
Current decarbonization options either cost too much (green hydrogen) or reduce emissions without generating revenue.
3
Capital Inefficiency
₹47 lakh crore estimated capex for India's sectors to reach net-zero. Long payback. Structural financial pressure.
The Answer

BIOLOOP: Co-Located Carbon Utilization

Place a biorefinery at the CO₂ source. Convert emissions into high-value biomass for industrial customers.

BIOLOOP deploys closed-loop photobioreactors at industrial facilities (cement, steel, power plants) to capture direct CO₂ emissions and convert them into standardized microalgal biomass.

The biomass is sold to downstream manufacturers of phycocyanin, protein ingredients, natural pigments, and aquaculture feed — creating a multi-revenue model that de-risks commodity pricing.

  • ✓ Zero CO₂ sourcing cost: Uses partner's direct flue gas (free input)
  • ✓ Energy self-sufficient: 60%+ powered by waste heat + biogas recovery
  • ✓ Land minimized: Closed PBRs use 10× less land than traditional algae
  • ✓ Multi-stream revenue: Biomass + carbon credits + operational margins
  • ✓ 15-year contracts: Partner-funded capex, recurring revenue model
🔄
Industrial CO₂ → Biomass
Closed photobioreactors convert direct flue gas into standardized microalgal biomass. Zero standalone infrastructure. 10 Ha facility = 2–4 MT/year production.
Technical Foundation

Proven Science. Integrated Engineering.

Every component is validated. The innovation is systems-level integration.

🧬 Biological Foundation
Microalgae cultivation is peer-reviewed and operationally proven at commercial scale (Spirulina, Chlorella globally; Haematococcus astaxanthin at Cyanotech, Algalif).
⚙️ Process Integration
Co-location with industrial partner eliminates capex barriers (no CO₂ sourcing, waste heat recovered, site provided) and reduces unit economics by 70–80%.
♻️ Energy Efficiency
Closed PBRs + flocculation harvesting + waste heat recovery + biogas CHP = EROI 1.5–2.5× (vs 0.13–0.71× for standalone systems).
📦 Market Ready
Biomass sold as standardized intermediate to downstream processors. No dependency on single market. Aquaculture, pharma, food ingredients all viable.

Execution Plan

From Pilot to Scale

Validated milestones. Clear decision gates. De-risked capital allocation.

Months 0–6
Phase 1: Lab Validation
Confirm strain performance, flocculation energy efficiency, and biomass yield targets.
  • ICGEB strain optimization (3+ cycles)
  • Flocculation energy validation (target: 0.5 kWh/kg)
  • HTL batch processing (6+ runs)
Months 6–18
Phase 2: Pilot Deployment
Construct and operationalize 10 Ha facility at industrial partner site.
  • Facility build (4–6 months)
  • 5+ harvest cycles (capture seasonal variance)
  • Buyer engagement (secure LOIs)
  • BEE carbon registration
Months 18–36
Phase 3: Commercial Ramp
First revenue realization. Second site identified. Playbook refinement.
  • Revenue achievement (₹10–50L Year 1)
  • Break-even validation (3–5 year projection)
  • Site 2 MoU signed
  • Series A preparation
Year 4+
Phase 4: Network Scale
5–10 operational sites. ₹200–400L recurring annual revenue.
  • Multi-site deployment (cement, steel, power)
  • Regional distribution established
  • Technology platform leadership
